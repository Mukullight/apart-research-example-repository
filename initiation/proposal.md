## 1. Executive Summary
The AI Safety Fellowship aims to accelerate the development of a robust AI safety talent pipeline by providing early-career researchers with funding, mentorship, and a collaborative community. This proposal outlines the need, high-level objectives, and a preliminary scope for initiating the fellowship program.

## 2. Problem Statement
As AI systems become more capable, the demand for rigorous safety research far outpaces the supply of trained practitioners. Existing PhD and grant programs are either too slow or too narrow in scope. An agile, globally accessible fellowship is required to:
- Bridge the gap between academic training and applied safety work.
- Foster interdisciplinary collaboration (ML, philosophy, policy, cybersecurity).
- Lower barriers for underrepresented groups to enter the field.

## 3. Proposed Solution
Launch a **6-month, cohort-based AI Safety Fellowship** that provides:
- A living stipend and research budget.
- 1:1 mentorship from senior safety researchers.
- Access to compute resources and curated learning paths.
- A community of peers and alumni.

## 4. High-Level Objectives
- Recruit and support 15–20 Fellows per cohort.
- Produce high-quality, publicly available safety research or tools.
- Establish a self-sustaining network of mentors and alumni.
- Secure multi-year funding commitments from aligned donors.

## 5. Scope & Boundaries
**In Scope:**
- Design of fellowship curriculum and selection criteria.
- Fundraising and sponsorship management.
- Operational setup (legal, finance, platform).
- Marketing and outreach to target candidates.

**Out of Scope (initially):**
- Hosting large-scale in-person events (virtual-first model).
- Direct funding of long-term institutional labs.
- Policy advocacy beyond research dissemination.

## 6. Key Risks & Mitigations
| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|------------|--------|------------|
| Insufficient quality applicants | Medium | High | Partner with universities and research groups; offer pre-fellowship workshops. |
| Mentor burnout / low engagement | High | Medium | Structured time commitments, honoraria, and training for mentors. |
| Funding shortfall | Medium | Critical | Phased launch with pilot cohort; maintain a reserve fund. |

## 7. Estimated Timeline
- **Month 1-2:** Secure seed funding and establish core team.
- **Month 3:** Design program structure and open call for mentors.
- **Month 4:** Launch application portal and marketing campaign.
- **Month 5:** Select pilot cohort (10 Fellows).
- **Month 6:** Begin pilot, gather feedback.
- **Month 7+:** Iterate and scale.

## 8. Recommendation
The proposal is recommended for advancement to a formal feasibility study and, upon successful evaluation, to a full project charter. Approval is sought to allocate initial resources for detailed planning.
