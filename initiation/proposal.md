## 1. Executive Summary
This proposal recommends launching a **remote, part‑time AI Safety Fellowship** modelled on the successful Apart Research fellowship template. The program will provide early‑career researchers with a structured environment to produce high‑quality safety research under mentorship, fostering a global talent pipeline and generating public‑good outputs.

## 2. Problem Statement
The field of AI safety faces a critical scarcity of skilled researchers. Traditional academic routes are slow, while full‑time programs exclude individuals with parallel commitments. Apart Research demonstrated that a lean, part‑time, remote fellowship can:

- Scale globally with low overhead.
- Produce publishable safety research in 12 weeks.
- Attract diverse talent otherwise lost to the field.

Replicating this model would immediately amplify our impact without reinventing the wheel.

## 3. Proposed Solution
Launch a **12‑week, part‑time (10‑15 hrs/week), remote AI Safety Fellowship** that mirrors the Apart Research approach:
- **Cohort size:** 20‑30 fellows.
- **Structure:** Weekly seminars, co‑working sessions, 1‑on‑1 mentorship.
- **Compensation:** Tiered stipend (e.g., $500–$2,000 depending on region and need).
- **Output:** A research paper or technical report, presented at a final symposium.
- **Infrastructure:** Lightweight toolset (Zoom, Slack, GitHub, Notion) with minimal custom development.

## 4. High‑Level Objectives
- Recruit and support 25 fellows per cohort, running two cohorts per year.
- Achieve ≥80% paper/publication rate from each cohort.
- Maintain a Net Promoter Score ≥ 50 from fellows and mentors.
- Become financially self‑sustaining within 3 cohorts via donor and institutional support.

## 5. Scope & Boundaries
**In Scope:**
- Fellowship design (curriculum, mentorship structure, selection criteria).
- Stipend disbursement and research support.
- Mentor recruitment, training, and management.
- Marketing, application, and selection logistics.
- Community building and alumni engagement.

**Out of Scope (initial phase):**
- Academic accreditation or formal university credits.
- Full‑time employment or long‑term contracts for fellows.
- Large‑scale in‑person events (retreats, conferences) — initially virtual‑only.

## 6. Key Risks & Mitigations
| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|------------|--------|------------|
| Low application quality | Medium | High | Targeted outreach through existing safety communities; pre‑fellowship workshops. |
| Mentor burnout | High | Medium | Honoraria, clear time commitment (1 hr/week), mentor rotation, and community recognition. |
| Insufficient funding | Medium | Critical | Phased launch with a pilot cohort; secure 80% funding before launch; tiered stipend model to control costs. |
| Operational overwhelm | Medium | Medium | Leverage proven processes from Apart Research playbook; hire dedicated program coordinator. |

## 7. Estimated Timeline
- **Month 1‑2:** Secure seed funding and program manager.
- **Month 3:** Adapt Apart Research open‑source curricula; finalize mentor contracts.
- **Month 4:** Open application portal and begin marketing.
- **Month 5:** Select and onboard pilot cohort (15 fellows).
- **Month 6‑9:** Execute pilot program.
- **Month 10:** Evaluate pilot, publish report, and launch second cohort.

## 8. Recommendation
Given the proven success and low‑risk profile of the Apart Research model, we recommend immediate approval to proceed with a feasibility study and subsequent charter.
