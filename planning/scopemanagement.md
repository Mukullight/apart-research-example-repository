# Scope Management Plan: AI Safety Fellowship (Apart Research Model)

**Project:** AI Safety Fellowship – Pilot Cohort  
**Date:** 2026-06-11  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Author:** Program Lead  

---

## 1. Purpose
This plan defines how the project scope will be developed, documented, verified, and controlled. It ensures that all necessary work—and only necessary work—is done to successfully deliver the 12‑week, part‑time remote pilot, modelled on the **Apart Research fellowship** approach.

## 2. Scope Definition
The project scope is derived from the approved Project Charter and Feasibility Study. The scope statement includes:
- Adaptation of the Apart Research fellowship playbook (curriculum, seminars, co‑working, mentorship).
- Recruitment of 8 mentors and selection of 15 fellows.
- Delivery of a 12‑week program (weekly seminars, co‑working, 1‑on‑1 mentorship).
- Tiered stipend disbursement for fellows, honoraria for mentors.
- Final symposium with publicly released research papers/reports.
- Post‑program evaluation and funding proposal for the next cohort.

**Exclusions:**
- In‑person events, academic accreditation, visa sponsorship, long‑term employment, or institutional lab funding.

## 3. Work Breakdown Structure (WBS)
Detailed in `work-breakdown-structure.md`. High‑level structure:
1. Program Setup & Adaptation  
2. Outreach & Selection  
3. Fellowship Execution & Support  
4. Evaluation & Close‑out  

## 4. Scope Verification & Acceptance
- **Deliverable Acceptance:** Key deliverables (adapted playbook, mentor contracts, fellow research papers, final report) are accepted by the Project Sponsor against predefined criteria.
- **Pilot Success Criteria:** Verified via NPS scores, paper submission rate (≥80%), retention rate (>85%), and funding commitments for the next cohort.

## 5. Scope Control
- **Change Requests:** Any addition, modification, or deletion of scope must be submitted via a formal Change Request Form.
- **Change Control Board (CCB):** Sponsor + Program Lead + one senior mentor. They review requests bi‑weekly.
- **Scope Creep Prevention:** The Program Lead holds the scope baseline (WBS, WBS dictionary, scope statement) and rejects any work not aligned with it during weekly team meetings.

## 6. Roles & Responsibilities
| Role | Scope Responsibility |
|------|----------------------|
| Project Sponsor | Approves scope baseline and any changes with budget/risk impact >5%. |
| Program Lead | Maintains scope baseline, manages change requests, ensures team adherence. |
| Operations Coordinator | Executes work packages, raises scope concerns immediately. |
| Fellows & Mentors | Provide feedback; do not control scope. |
