## 2. Project Scope Management
**Scope Statement:**  
Design and deliver a 12‑week, part‑time (10‑15 hrs/week), remote AI Safety Fellowship for 15 fellows, guided by 8 mentors, producing publishable research papers. Stipends are tiered; all activities are virtual. Exclusions: academic credit, visa sponsorship, in‑person events, long‑term employment.

**WBS:**  
High‑level phases: 1.1 Program Setup & Adaptation, 1.2 Outreach & Selection, 1.3 Fellowship Execution & Support, 1.4 Evaluation & Close‑out. Full WBS and dictionary are in `work-breakdown-structure.md`.

**Scope Control:**  
Any addition, modification, or deletion of scope requires a formal Change Request. The Change Control Board (Sponsor + Program Lead + one senior mentor) reviews requests bi‑weekly. The Program Lead maintains the scope baseline and prevents scope creep through weekly team reviews.

## 5. Quality Management
**Quality Objectives:**
- ≥80% of fellows submit a research paper to a workshop or journal.
- Retention rate >85%.
- Fellow NPS ≥ 50.
- Mentor NPS ≥ 50.
- 100% on‑time stipend and honoraria payments.

**Quality Assurance:**  
- Program design is based on the proven Apart Research playbook, which is audited against past cohort outcomes.  
- Mentor training ensures consistent guidance.  
- Weekly pulse surveys and mid‑program check‑in provide leading indicators.

**Quality Control:**  
- Research papers are reviewed by at least one mentor and one peer before symposium.  
- Payment transactions are verified against the tier schedule by the fiscal sponsor.  
- Final metrics are validated by an independent reviewer (Advisory Board member).

## 10. Change Management
Any proposed change to the project scope, schedule, cost baseline, or quality targets must follow the Change Control Process:

1. **Identify & Document:** Change Requester fills out a Change Request Form (in Notion), describing the change, rationale, and impact.
2. **Review:** Program Lead reviews for completeness and initial impact assessment.
3. **CCB Decision:** The Change Control Board (Sponsor + Program Lead + Senior Mentor) evaluates the request. Decision criteria include alignment with objectives, budget/schedule impact, and risk.
4. **Implement:** If approved, the Program Lead updates all affected baselines and communicates the change to relevant stakeholders.
5. **Log:** All changes are recorded in the Change Log.

**Escalation:** If CCB cannot reach consensus, the Sponsor has final authority.
