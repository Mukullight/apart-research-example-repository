# Procurement Management Plan: AI Safety Fellowship (Apart Research Model)

**Project:** AI Safety Fellowship – Pilot Cohort  
**Date:** 2026-06-11  
**Version:** 1.0  

---

## 1. Purpose
This plan details how goods and services will be acquired externally for the pilot. Because the fellowship model is intentionally lightweight (following Apart Research’s lean approach), procurement is minimal and mostly limited to individual contracts and off‑the‑shelf software.

## 2. Make‑or‑Buy Analysis
| Item / Service | Decision | Rationale |
|----------------|----------|-----------|
| Curriculum & playbook adaptation | Make (in‑house) | Based on open‑source materials; customised by Program Lead. |
| Mentor services | Buy (individual contractors) | External expertise required; honoraria contracts. |
| Cloud compute for fellows | Buy (in‑kind or small cash) | Apply for Google Cloud credits; if not, small purchases. |
| Marketing design (social media assets) | Buy (freelance) | One‑time need; low cost. |
| Software tools (Zoom, Airtable, Slack) | Buy (subscriptions) | Standard SaaS; free tiers may suffice, but budget includes pro plans. |
| Stipend payment processing | Make (use existing) | Fiscal sponsor’s accounts payable; no extra cost. |

## 3. Procurement Items & Contract Types
| Item ID | Description | Est. Value | Contract Type | Source Selection |
|---------|-------------|------------|---------------|------------------|
| PROC‑01 | 8 Mentor honoraria contracts | Fixed‑price | Direct invitation based on expertise |
| PROC‑02 | Freelance graphic designer (assets) | Fixed‑price (deliverable) | Competitive quotes (2‑3 freelancers via Upwork) |
| PROC‑03 | Cloud credits (if not in‑kind) | Purchase order | Direct from provider |
| PROC‑04 | Software subscriptions (Zoom Pro, Airtable Pro) | Fixed‑price annual/monthly | Direct online purchase |

## 4. Procurement Process
1. **Need identification:** Program Lead flags requirement.  
2. **Approval:** Sponsor approves any procurement > y.  
3. **Solicitation:** For freelancer, a brief is shared; for mentors, a standard agreement is used.  
4. **Selection:** Based on qualifications, past work, and cost (where applicable).  
5. **Contracting:** Signed agreement with deliverables, timeline, and payment terms.  
6. **Management & Closure:** Work is monitored; upon acceptance, payment released and contract closed.

## 5. Contract Management
- **Mentors:** Managed by Mentorship Coordinator; performance tracked via fellow feedback.  
- **Freelancer:** Managed by Program Lead; milestone payment upon delivery of final assets.  
- **Software subscriptions:** Auto‑renewal monitored; cancel when not needed.

## 6. Procurement Risks & Mitigations
| Risk | Response |
|------|----------|
| Mentor drops out before program | Maintain a backup list of 2‑3 qualified researchers; contracts include a 2‑week notice clause. |
| Cloud credits denied | Purchase small‑scale credits from budget; fellows can also use free Google Colab. |
| Freelancer under‑delivers | Set clear milestones; retain final payment until acceptance. |

## 7. Roles & Responsibilities
| Role | Responsibility |
|------|----------------|
| Program Lead | Overall procurement management, approves small purchases, manages freelancer. |
| Mentorship Coordinator | Manages mentor contracts and performance. |
| Finance/Ops | Processes payments, maintains procurement documentation. |
| Fiscal Sponsor | Reviews contracts > s; ensures compliance. |

---

*All procurement must comply with the fiscal sponsor’s purchasing policy.*
