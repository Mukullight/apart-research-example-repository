# Project Closure Report: AI Safety Fellowship (Pilot)

**Project:** AI Safety Fellowship – Pilot Cohort (Apart Research Model)  
**Date:** 2026-01-30  
**Author:** Program Lead  
**Approval:** Project Sponsor  

---

## 1. Project Summary
The pilot cohort of the AI Safety Fellowship ran from **15 October 2026 to 15 January 2027** as a 12‑week, part‑time, remote program. It enrolled **15 early‑career researchers**, supported by **8 experienced mentors**, following a proven model adapted from Apart Research. The project was executed under the Project Management Plan (PMP v1.0) and funded by a $50,000 seed donation.

## 2. Final Status
- **Overall Verdict:** **Success** – all key objectives were met or exceeded.
- **Schedule:** Completed on time (within 0.5‑day variance).
- **Budget:** Final spend $42,180, under the $43,450 baseline (CPI = 1.03).
- **Scope:** All planned deliverables accepted.
- **Quality:** Exceeded target for research output and satisfaction.

## 3. Transition & Handover
- Program assets (playbook, templates, Slack community) transferred to the permanent Fellowship Program Manager for Cohort 2.
- All financial records closed and reconciled with the fiscal sponsor.
- Donor final report submitted.
- Alumni network and mentor relationships maintained for future cohorts.

## 4. Benefits Realised
- 14 fellows (93%) completed the program and submitted research papers.
- 5 papers accepted at workshops, 3 more submitted to journals.
- Stipends paid 100% on time; zero compliance issues.
- Strong community formed; 8 fellows have joined the alumni mentor pool.

## 5. Lessons Learned
| Category | What Went Well | What Could Be Improved |
|----------|----------------|------------------------|
| Recruitment | Targeted outreach to AI safety groups attracted high-quality applicants. | Application window could be 1 week longer for underrepresented regions. |
| Mentorship | 1‑on‑1 model highly valued; honoraria appreciated. | Provide more mentor training on supporting diverse fellows. |
| Program Delivery | Weekly seminars and co‑working built strong momentum. | Introduce more structured peer‑review before final symposium. |
| Operations | Lean tools (Zoom, Slack, Notion) sufficient; no major tech issues. | Automate stipend tier verification for scaling. |

## 6. Formal Acceptance
The Sponsor accepts the completed project, confirms all objectives met, and authorises administrative closure. All documentation will be archived in the organisation’s knowledge repository.
